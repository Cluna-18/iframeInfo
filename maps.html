Google Maps in iframes


We can also use iframes to embed something like google maps in to webpage.

Here is an example of an a google maps iframe, centered around our campus. Note that we can still interact with and scroll around on this map like usual.

Note also that in this iframe we have some additional special features as well, such as a further embedded link "view larger map" which links to the full google maps page, or the panel in the bottom left that allows you to switch map styles. These additional features are a result of the way google maps generates the iframe code. Similar to youtube, we cannot simply copy the link and paste in in to the "src" attribute of the iframe. Rather, there are steps we must take in google maps to attain the correct code to embed a maps page in an iframe. Here are the steps:

  1. Go to the google maps page you wish to embed
  2. In the search bar in the upper left, where you would typically put your desired location, click the three horizontal lines on the left to open a menu panel
  3. Click on the "share or embed map" option
  4. You will be brought first to the "Send a link" tab. Click instead on the "embed a map" tab.
  5. You will now see the HTML code as well as a preview of what the embedding will look like. Click "COPY HTML"
  6. Paste the code directly in to your HTML file.

The code will look something like this:
